Paper Airplane Challenge

Lesson 1.2: Engineering Design in Action

✈️

Design, Build, Test, Improve!

Lesson 1.2 | 50 minutes + homework

Today's Learning Objectives

By the end of today, you will:

  • Apply all 7 steps of the Engineering Design Process
  • Design a paper airplane for a specific goal (distance OR accuracy)
  • Build and test multiple prototypes
  • Collect and analyze test data
  • Make iterative improvements based on testing
  • Document your process in an engineering journal

Real engineering, real iteration, real fun!

The Challenge

Choose Your Mission:

Option A: Distance

🎯

Design a plane that flies the farthest distance

Option B: Accuracy

🎪

Design a plane that flies through a target hoop

Materials:

  • Standard 8.5" × 11" paper (multiple sheets)
  • Optional: tape, paperclips (ask first!)

1 Ask: Understand the Challenge

What do you need to understand?

  • Distance mission: What makes a plane fly far? (stability, weight, aerodynamics)
  • Accuracy mission: What makes a plane controllable? (balance, predictability)
  • What are the constraints? (materials, time, testing space)

Key Questions

  • What goal did you choose?
  • What matters most for YOUR goal?
  • What have you seen work before?

Activity (5 min)

Discuss with a partner: What features do you think will help you succeed?

2 Research: Gather Information

Research Activities

  • Look at example plane designs
  • Think about planes you've made before
  • What worked? What didn't?
  • Consider: wing shape, weight distribution, nose design

Key Concepts

  • Lift: Upward force from air
  • Drag: Air resistance
  • Weight: Gravity pulling down
  • Thrust: Your throwing force

3 Imagine: Brainstorm Designs

Brainstorming Rules

  • No bad ideas! Get creative!
  • Sketch at least 3 different designs
  • Think about different wing shapes, nose types, and folds
  • Consider what will help YOUR specific goal

Distance Design Ideas

  • Long, narrow wings for gliding
  • Pointed nose for aerodynamics
  • Weight forward for stability
  • Smooth, simple design

Accuracy Design Ideas

  • Wide wings for control
  • Balanced weight distribution
  • Fins for stability
  • Predictable flight path

Activity (10 min): Sketch 3 different airplane designs in your journal

4 Plan: Choose Your Best Design

Selection Criteria

Look at your 3 sketches. Which one will work best for your goal?

Evaluate Each Design

  • Does it match my goal?
  • Can I actually build it?
  • Will it be stable?
  • Is it too complex?

Activity (5 min)

Circle your chosen design and write 2-3 sentences explaining WHY you chose it

Your Build Plan

  • What folds will you make first?
  • Where will you add weight (if needed)?
  • How will you test it?

5 Create: Build Your Prototype

Build Time!

Carefully fold your chosen design. Take your time!

Building Tips

  • Make crisp, clean folds
  • Keep wings symmetrical
  • Check balance before testing
  • Make adjustments as needed

Activity (10 min)

Prototype 1: Build your first airplane following your plan

Label it "V1" (Version 1)

Remember: This is just your FIRST prototype. You'll make improvements!

6 Test: Gather Data

Testing Protocol

  • Test each plane 3 times
  • Record the results each time
  • Take notes on what you observe

Distance Mission

Measure in feet/meters from throw line to where it lands

Trial 1: _____
Trial 2: _____
Trial 3: _____
Average: _____

Accuracy Mission

Did it go through the hoop? (Yes/No)

Trial 1: _____
Trial 2: _____
Trial 3: _____
Success: __ / 3

Activity (10 min): Test your V1 plane and record results in your journal

7 Improve: Make It Better!

Analyze Your Results

What worked? What didn't? Why?

Common Issues & Fixes

  • Dives down: Move weight back or adjust wings up
  • Flies up then stalls: Add weight to nose
  • Veers left/right: Check wing symmetry
  • Wobbly: Stiffen wings or add tail

Iteration Activity

Build an improved V2 (or V3!) based on what you learned

Document your changes and test again!

This is REAL engineering! Professionals iterate many times to get the best design

Final Competition

Test Your Final Design!

Use your best version for the final competition

🏆

Distance Competition

Who can fly the farthest?

🎯

Accuracy Competition

Who can hit the target?

Record Your Final Results

  • Final version number (V2, V3, etc.)
  • Final test results
  • Competition placement

Homework: Engineering Journal Entry

📓 Journal Assignment (Due Next Class)

Write a complete reflection on your engineering process

Required Sections:

  • Ask: What goal did you choose? Why?
  • Research: What did you learn about airplane design?
  • Imagine: Describe your 3 sketched designs
  • Plan: Which design did you choose? Why?
  • Create: How many versions did you build?
  • Test: Include all your test data (V1, V2, V3, etc.)
  • Improve: What changes did you make? Why? What worked?
  • Reflection: What did you learn about the engineering design process?

Length: 1-2 pages, typed or handwritten (neat and legible!)
