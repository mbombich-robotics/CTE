Arrival & Baggage Claim — Terminal 2E

Delta arrivals use Terminal 2E. Depending on your specific gate (Hall K, L, or M), you may need to ride the free CDGVAL shuttle train to reach passport control and baggage claim, which are centered on Hall K, Level 0.

French signage you'll see, in order

Contrôle des passeports / Contrôle frontière Passport control Right after leaving the jet bridge
Autres passeports / All Passports Non-EU/non-French line (this is your line) At passport control — don't queue in the EU line

Correspondance Flight connections Not relevant to you, but common on overhead signs

Order of operations: exit jet bridge → follow overhead signs (shuttle train if needed) → passport control, "Autres Passeports" line → baggage claim (match your carousel to your flight number on the screens) → Douane/customs, green channel if you have nothing to declare → Sortie (exit).

Rental Car — Sixt

  • Location: Sixt's counter is in the building connecting Terminal 2E and Terminal 2F, on the Arrivals level — not a separate off-site lot.
  • From baggage claim, follow signs for "Location de voitures / Car Rental" toward the exit; the car rental hall is a short, signed walk from the T2E arrivals area.
  • Counter hours are typically 6:00 AM–11:00 PM, so a 6:45 AM landing is well within their staffed hours.
  • After paperwork, you'll take an elevator or escalator down to the pickup garage (part of the "Parking EF" structure) to get the car.

Budget about 45–60 minutes total from baggage claim to driving out of the garage, including a possible line at the counter.

Road Directions: CDG → Versailles → Tanis

  • Leaving the Sixt garage, follow signs for "Toutes Directions" / A1 toward Paris.
  • Pick up A104, then A86 (the ring highway around Paris) heading toward Versailles / Saint-Quentin-en-Yvelines — this detour only adds about 15 minutes over heading straight out to the old Normandy route, since CDG → Versailles is itself just a 49-minute, 30-mile drive.
  • Exit A86 for Versailles / Château de Versailles; signs lead directly to the Place d'Armes car park at the palace gates.
  • Leaving Versailles, pick up A13 toward Rouen/Caen, then A84 toward Avranches, exiting for local roads into Tanis.

Set your GPS to "Château de Versailles" as the first waypoint, then "Tanis" for the afternoon leg — the above is just the highway skeleton so you know what to look for on the signs. Versailles → Tanis is about 4 hours, mostly A13/A84, so budget for a full driving day on the back end.

Tips & Notes

  • Book Passport tickets online in advance and reserve a Palace timed-entry slot — walk-up lines can run long, and slots (hourly, 9 AM–3 PM) can sell out on busy summer Sundays.
  • Today is a Sunday, so the Grandes Eaux Musicales fountain show is running and already included in the Passport ticket — worth timing the gardens visit around it.
  • This is a jet-lag day on top of a big attraction — keep the schedule loose. If anyone needs to sit down and rest, do it; the Trianon Estate is the easiest thing to skip if you're running behind, since the Palace and main gardens are the priority.
  • The drive from Versailles to Tanis is a real 4 hours — stop for a break if needed rather than pushing straight through after an overnight flight and a full day of walking.
