Vicksburg High School currently offers a variety of Career and Technical Education (CTE) courses, including Zoology, Botany, Wildlife, and a variety of business classes. Vicksburg Middle School provides foundational exposure to STEM through its science and exploratory programs. The intent of this new course is to further develop students’ understanding of engineering, programming, and automation principles through hands-on, project-based learning in the field of robotics. The robotics curriculum will be vertically aligned with STEM offerings at both VHS and VMS to ensure consistency and growth in student learning and skill development.

This proposed new high school course gives students an opportunity to earn an elective credit in the CTE/STEM department, offering a rigorous and engaging option for students interested in robotics, coding, design, and problem-solving. It will serve as a stepping stone toward more advanced STEM and engineering coursework.  

Additionally, this course will enhance the alignment of the STEM pathway by allowing additional content following foundational STEM experiences in middle school and introductory technology courses in high school. This course will address the following concerns/issues:

Design and Build Lab will create a more engaging, skill-based, and future-ready learning environment for students. It will complement Applied Engineering and Robotics (CTE) while reinforcing VHS’s vision that all students will graduate college and career-ready.

Design and Build Lab (CTE) Course Description

Grade Level: 9, 10, 11, 12 (Full year/semester)

Turn ideas into real, professional quality creations using modern fabrication tools and smart tech. In this hands-on class, you will use the Engineering Design Process (EDP) to move from sketch to finished product with CAD software, computer programming, 3D printers, and CNC tools.  Each unit builds your skills in design principles, tool operation, and coding, then gives you the freedom to create your own projects.  By year’s end, you will have a strong portfolio of high-quality fabricated work and the skills needed for future pathways in engineering, design, or tech.  

Prerequisite (High School): None

HIGH SCHOOL: Addition of Design and Build Lab (CTE) elective class as an option for students who are interested in hands-on, project-based learning in the fields of robotics, automation, and engineering.

The Design and Build Lab course provides students with hands-on, project-based learning experiences in engineering design, fabrication, and emerging technologies. Designed for creative problem-solvers and future innovators, this class emphasizes the Engineering Design Process (EDP) to take projects from concept to completion.

Students will gain real-world experience using CAD software, 3D printers, CNC routers and plasma cutters, laser engravers, and coding tools to design and build functional products and prototypes.

By the end of the course, students will:

  • Apply the engineering design process to develop innovative products and solutions.
  • Use digital fabrication tools and manufacturing techniques safely and effectively.
  • Create and refine custom projects such as décor, gadgets, jewelry, or prototypes.
  • Develop basic skills in computer coding and automation concepts.
  • Work collaboratively to design, test, and present completed builds.
  • Build a professional portfolio highlighting design and fabrication skills.

No traditional textbook is required — learning is driven through hands-on projects, teacher-created materials, online resources, and real-world tools used in modern engineering and design careers.

Credit: CTE Elective Credit (Design and Build Lab)

Proposed Topics/Syllabus - HIGH SCHOOL COURSE

Unit

Unit Title

Unit Overview

Topics

1

Foundations in Safety and Design

This unit introduces students to foundational safety practices and prepares them for hands-on work in the lab environment.

  • Workplace safety regulations (OSHA standards)
  • Proper use of tools, machinery, and personal protective equipment (PPE)
  • Material handling and hazard awareness
  • Lab orientation and safety protocols
  • Safety training modules and simulations

2

Introduction to Engineering Design

In this unit, students explore the basics of the engineering design process and learn to approach projects with creativity and problem-solving skills.

  • Engineering Design Process (EDP) overview
  • Project planning and brainstorming techniques
  • Problem-solving and iterative design cycles
  • Team roles and collaboration strategies
  • Case studies of real-world design projects

3

CAD and Digital Modeling

This unit introduces students to digital design tools and modeling techniques. Students apply skills through Project 1: 3D Printed Fidget Spinner - designing and printing a functional fidget spinner with bearings.

  • Basics of CAD software (OnShape, SolidWorks, Autodesk)
  • 2D and 3D modeling, sketching, and prototyping
  • Exporting files for 3D printing (STL, G-code)
  • Project 1: Design fidget spinner in CAD, incorporate 608 bearings
  • 3D printer setup, slicing software, print optimization

4

Introduction to Fabrication Tools

Students gain hands-on experience with fabrication tools through Project 2: Custom Acrylic Ornament (laser engraving) and Project 3: Silicone Molds (3D printing mold masters and silicone casting).

  • Laser engraving/cutting techniques and safety
  • Project 2: Design and laser-engrave custom acrylic ornaments with LED bases
  • 3D printing for mold masters and tooling
  • Project 3: Create silicone molds from 3D printed masters
  • Material selection, mold release, and casting techniques

5

Coding and Automation Basics

This unit introduces programming and automation through Project 4: Automated Plant Watering System - students build Arduino-based systems with soil moisture sensors and water pumps.

  • Arduino programming fundamentals (C/C++)
  • Capacitive soil moisture sensors and calibration
  • Relay modules, water pumps, and power management
  • Project 4: Build automated plant watering system with enclosure
  • Circuit design, wiring, and troubleshooting

6

Advanced Fabrication and Systems Integration

Students apply multiple fabrication skills through Project 5: LED Sign/Wall Art - a team project combining CNC/plasma cutting, laser engraving, LED wiring, and soldering into a finished illuminated display piece.

  • CNC router and plasma cutter operation
  • LED strip wiring, soldering, and 12V power systems
  • Project 5: Design and build illuminated wall art/signage
  • Multi-material assembly (wood, acrylic, metal)
  • Team collaboration and project management

7

Capstone Project Development

Project 6: Capstone Project - Teams design and develop an original project that integrates skills from all previous units. Students utilize existing consumables and lab equipment to create a functional product or prototype.

  • Project 6: Open-ended capstone with team-selected scope
  • Project proposal, timeline, and resource planning
  • Design iteration, prototyping, and testing
  • Integration of CAD, fabrication, electronics, and/or coding
  • Documentation, portfolio, and presentation preparation

8

Project Presentation and Reflection

This unit allows students to present their work, reflect on their learning, and evaluate team performance.

  • Final project presentation to peers or community
  • Demonstration of functional prototypes
  • Reflection on design process and lessons learned
  • Peer and self-evaluation
  • Portfolio completion and skills assessment
