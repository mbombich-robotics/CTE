Staying Safe in the Shop

Top 5 Shop Rules

  • Always wear required PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)
  • No loose clothing or jewelry
  • Closed-toed shoes required
  • Don't touch machines you're not trained on

The Engineering Design Process

A systematic approach to solving problems

1. Ask

Empathize & understand user needs

2. Research

Clarify the problem

3. Imagine

Brainstorm solutions

4. Plan

Select a promising solution

5. Create

Build a prototype

6. Test

Gather feedback

7. Improve

Refine iterations of your design

Poll: Which step seems most critical to you?

Step 1: Ask – Empathize & Understand

Purpose

Put yourself in the user's shoes

Key Activities

  • Interview stakeholders (customers, end users, anyone who will have input on the final product)
  • Observe behaviors & pain points
  • Create an empathy map (What they say, think, feel, do)

Prompt Question

"What really matters to our user?"

Step 2: Research – Clarify the Problem

Purpose

Gather facts & define the scope

Key Activities

  • Review incident reports / existing designs
  • List requirements & constraints (size, cost, safety)
  • Benchmark similar solutions

Prompt Question

"What do we know – and what don't we know?"

Step 3: Imagine – Brainstorm Solutions

Purpose

Generate as many ideas as possible

Key Activities

  • Brainwriting / mind-mapping sessions
  • Crazy-8 sketch exercise (8 sketches × 8 minutes)
  • No-judgment "Yes, and…" culture

Prompt Question

"How might we…?"

Step 4: Plan – Select a Promising Solution

Purpose

Narrow down to the best concept

Key Activities

  • Evaluate ideas versus criteria (Pugh matrix)
  • Identify materials, tools, timeline, and roles
  • Sketch the key features & assembly steps

Prompt Question

"Which concept best meets our needs?"

Step 5: Create – Build a Prototype

Purpose

Turn your plan into a tangible model

Key Activities

  • Low-fidelity mock-ups (cardboard, foam, clay)
  • CAD model export → 3D print / laser-cut parts
  • Assemble and document build steps

Prompt Question

"Does it look and feel like we expected?"

Step 6: Test – Gather Feedback

Purpose

See how your prototype performs

Key Activities

  • Define test criteria (strength, fit, function)
  • Conduct user trials / performance runs
  • Record data, observations, and "failure modes"

Prompt Question

"What worked, and what didn't?"

Step 7: Improve – Refine & Iterate

Purpose

Use test insights to enhance your design

Key Activities

  • Update CAD models or prototype builds
  • Retest the modified version
  • Track version history & lessons learned

Prompt Question

"How can we make it even better?"

The Process is Iterative

Engineering design is a cycle, not a straight line!

You can jump between steps as you learn and discover new insights!
