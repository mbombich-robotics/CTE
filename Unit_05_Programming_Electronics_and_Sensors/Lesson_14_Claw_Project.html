The Challenge

Your claw must do this — automatically, every time:

📡 Serial trigger received
↓
🟢 Claw opens fully
↓ [place object]
⚙️ Claw closes slowly — feedback monitored each step
↓
🔵 Is feedback still changing?
YES → keep closing  |  NO → contact detected!
↓
💡 LED color = object size   (🟢 large   🔵 medium   🔴 small)
↓
🔍 Hold grip · monitor for slip · 🟡 flash if object moves

Why this is interesting

The claw can't see the object. It uses sensor feedback to know when it's touched something — the same principle used in industrial robot grippers.

What you'll learn

  • Servo PWM control
  • Analog feedback / ADC
  • Feedback-controlled loops
  • AI-assisted coding & documentation

AI in This Class

"If you can't explain it, you don't own it."

Rule 1 — Give Context

Tell AI who you are, what hardware you're using, and what you already know. Vague prompts give vague answers.

Rule 2 — Show Your Thinking

Share your plan first, then ask AI to help. AI improves your thinking better than it replaces it.

Rule 3 — Push Back

AI is wrong sometimes. If something doesn't make sense, say so. Accepting wrong answers is on you.

What AI use looks like here

  • Generate code — then annotate every line yourself
  • Explain concepts — then rewrite in your own words
  • Review your spec sheet — then verify what it changes
  • Log every prompt in your AI Log section

How you're graded

  • Spec sheet — accuracy & clarity, not polish
  • Live demo — code must work on real hardware
  • Quiz — everything in your spec sheet is fair game

Today: Assembly

First AI Prompt

Before you open the box, try this prompt:

"I am assembling a Pololu Micro Gripper Kit (product #3551). It uses a servo motor and a rack-and-pinion mechanism to move two parallel paddles. Give me step-by-step assembly instructions."

Then open the official Pololu guide and compare. Where did AI get it right? Where was it vague or wrong? Write this in your spec sheet.

Why this matters

AI doesn't have a camera in your kit. It may not know the exact order of assembly steps. Verifying AI output against a reliable source is an engineering skill — practice it now.

Assembly Checklist

  • Use the official Pololu assembly guide
  • Do not force any parts — the rack slides in one direction
  • The servo should be seated flush before securing
  • Three wires when done: signal (orange/yellow), power (red), ground (brown/black), feedback (white/extra)
