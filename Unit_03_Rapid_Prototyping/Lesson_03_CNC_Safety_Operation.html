🚨 CNC Safety & Operation

⚠️ CRITICAL SAFETY LESSON

CNC machines are powerful industrial equipment capable of causing serious injury or death. This lesson covers mandatory safety protocols. 100% attendance and quiz passage required to operate equipment.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify required PPE for CNC router and plasma cutter operation
  • Perform pre-operation safety checks
  • Recognize emergency stop procedures
  • Understand machine hazards and mitigation strategies
  • Follow safe material handling protocols
  • Pass the CNC safety certification quiz

Course Policy: Students who fail the safety quiz or violate safety protocols will lose CNC equipment privileges for the remainder of the semester. No exceptions.

⚡ The Big 3 CNC Hazards

🔄

1. ROTATING TOOLS

Spindle Speed: Up to 24,000 RPM (400 revolutions per second)

Hazard: Contact with spinning bit = instant severe laceration or amputation

Mitigation: NEVER reach into work area while spindle is running

🎯

2. FLYING DEBRIS

Chip Velocity: Up to 60 mph from cutting area

Hazard: Eye injury, burns, cuts from ejected material

Mitigation: Mandatory safety glasses + enclosure closed during operation

⚡

3. ELECTRICAL SHOCK

Voltage: 220V single-phase (plasma), 110V (router)

Hazard: Electrocution, arc flash burns (plasma)

Mitigation: Never touch electrical components during operation

ZERO-TOLERANCE RULE: Any student operating equipment without required PPE or with safety bypasses disabled will receive an immediate F for the course and disciplinary referral.

🥽 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

OMIO CNC Router - Required PPE

✅ Safety Glasses

ANSI Z87.1 rated, side shields required. No prescription glasses unless rated.

✅ Hearing Protection

Foam earplugs or earmuffs. Spindle noise: 85-95 dB (hearing damage zone).

✅ Closed-Toe Shoes

No sandals, crocs, or exposed toes. Steel-toe recommended for metal work.

✅ No Loose Clothing

Tie back long hair. No jewelry, lanyards, or loose sleeves. Tuck in shirts.

Crossfire Pro Plasma - Additional PPE

  • Welding Helmet: Shade #5 minimum for plasma arc (UV radiation hazard)
  • Leather Gloves: Heat-resistant for handling cut metal (600°F+)
  • Long Sleeves: Cotton or FR-rated to prevent arc burns
  • Respirator: Metal fume vapor can cause "metal fume fever"

✅ Pre-Operation Safety Checklist

BEFORE Every CNC Job

  • 1 Inspect Work Area: Clear of tools, debris, tripping hazards
  • 2 Verify Emergency Stop: Test E-stop button functionality
  • 3 Check Tool Security: Collet tight, tool properly seated (no wobble)
  • 4 Secure Material: Workpiece clamped/screwed down (will NOT move under cutting forces)
  • 5 Verify G-code: Correct file loaded, simulation looks right
  • 6 Set Zero Position: X, Y, Z zeros match toolpath expectations
  • 7 Clear Enclosure: No tools, hands, or objects in cutting path
  • 8 PPE Check: Glasses, hearing protection, appropriate clothing

NEVER SKIP STEPS: 90% of CNC accidents are caused by rushing through pre-flight checks. If you're not sure, ASK THE INSTRUCTOR.

🔩 Securing Material: The Right Way

Why Workholding Matters

A loose workpiece becomes a projectile at 24,000 RPM. Cutting forces can exceed 50 lbs—way stronger than friction alone.

Approved Workholding Methods

✅ Screws to Spoilboard

Best for polycarbonate decks. Screws in corners + center. Countersink screw heads below cut depth.

✅ Clamps + Toe Clamps

For thicker stock. Position clamps OUTSIDE toolpath. Verify in simulation.

✅ Vacuum Table (if available)

For thin sheet goods. Requires flat, non-porous material. Check vacuum gauge.

❌ NEVER Use Tape Alone

Double-sided tape is NOT sufficient for CNC routing. Only use tape + additional screws/clamps.

Plasma Workholding: Material must sit flat on slats. No clamping needed (no lateral force), but warped material causes dive/crash.

🚨 Emergency Stop Procedures

When to Hit the E-Stop

  • Tool breaks or comes loose during cut
  • Material starts to lift or move
  • Smoke, fire, or unusual burning smell
  • Someone enters the danger zone
  • Machine makes unexpected grinding/crashing sounds
  • Any situation that feels unsafe (trust your gut!)

E-Stop Protocol

1 HIT RED E-STOP BUTTON (large red mushroom button on controller)

2 Machine power cuts immediately (spindle coasts to stop)

3 DO NOT reach into work area until spindle is fully stopped

4 Notify instructor immediately

5 Do NOT reset E-stop or restart without instructor approval

False Alarm? That's okay! Better to stop the machine and ask than to risk injury. You will NEVER be penalized for using the E-stop in good faith.

🔥 Fire Prevention & Response

CNC Fire Hazards

  • Friction fires: Dull tools + high feed rates = heat buildup in plastics
  • Chip accumulation: Sawdust piles near hot spindle
  • Plasma sparks: Metal cutting generates 5000°F sparks
  • Electrical shorts: Damaged wiring or coolant leaks

Fire Response Protocol

  • 1 HIT E-STOP to cut machine power
  • 2 Alert instructor: Shout "FIRE!" loudly
  • 3 Evacuate area: Move away from machine
  • 4 If fire is small: Instructor will use ABC fire extinguisher
  • 5 If fire spreads: Evacuate building, pull fire alarm, call 911

Fire Extinguisher Locations:

  • Near shop entrance (ABC rated)
  • Welding area (ABC rated)
  • Plasma CNC table (metal fire extinguisher)

▶️ Safe Operation Procedures

During CNC Operation

  • NEVER leave machine unattended while program is running
  • Keep hands outside enclosure at all times during cutting
  • Monitor first layer/pass closely for proper Z-height and material adhesion
  • Watch for chip buildup that could interfere with tool path
  • Listen for unusual sounds (grinding, squealing, crashing)
  • Stay alert: No phones, headphones, or distractions

Feed Rate Override

Most CNC controllers have a feed rate override dial (usually 0-200%):

  • First run of new G-code: Start at 50% to verify toolpath
  • Normal operation: 100%
  • If tool sounds stressed: Reduce to 75%
  • NEVER exceed 100% unless instructor approves

🧹 Post-Operation Procedures

After Job Completes

  • 1 Let spindle stop completely before opening enclosure
  • 2 Verify machine is in idle state (no active G-code)
  • 3 Remove workpiece carefully—edges may be sharp!
  • 4 Vacuum work area (chips, sawdust, metal filings)
  • 5 Remove and clean tool (wipe with cotton cloth, return to tool holder)
  • 6 Return machine to home position
  • 7 Log job in machine notebook (date, material, runtime)
  • 8 Power down controller if last user

5S Cleanup Standard

Leave the machine cleaner than you found it:

  • Sort: Remove scrap and unused materials
  • Set in Order: Tools returned to proper locations
  • Shine: Vacuum table, wipe down enclosure
  • Standardize: Follow shutdown checklist
  • Sustain: Make it a habit
