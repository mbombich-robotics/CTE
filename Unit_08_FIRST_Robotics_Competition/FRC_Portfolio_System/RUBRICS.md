# FRC Portfolio - Grading Rubrics

## Weekly Reflection Rubric (20 points each)

| Category | Excellent (5 pts) | Good (4 pts) | Satisfactory (3 pts) | Needs Improvement (1-2 pts) | Missing (0 pts) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|----------------------|-----------------------------|-----------------|
| **Contributions** | 3+ specific tasks with dates; clear "I" statements; detailed descriptions | 3 tasks listed; mostly specific; uses "I" statements | 3 tasks but vague; some "we" statements | Fewer than 3 tasks; very vague; uses "we" throughout | Not submitted |
| **Evidence** | 2+ quality photos showing personal work; clear connection to contributions | 2 photos; shows work but connection unclear | 1 photo; minimal evidence | Photos don't show personal work | No evidence |
| **Challenges/Solutions** | Specific technical challenges; thoughtful problem-solving process | Identifies challenges; provides solutions | Generic challenges; basic solutions | Vague or missing challenges/solutions | Not addressed |
| **Goals** | 3 specific, measurable goals; realistic for one week | 3 goals; mostly specific | Goals present but vague | Fewer than 3 goals; unrealistic | No goals |

## Red Flags for "Coattail Riding"

Watch for these warning signs that a student may not be contributing:

1. **Vague reflections** - "Helped the team" instead of "I wired the limit switches on the intake"
2. **No personal photos** - Only group photos or robot photos without showing their work
3. **Cannot explain** - When asked to explain their subsystem, gives vague answers
4. **Copy-paste content** - Same text as teammates in reflections
5. **Always late** - Consistently behind teammates on submissions
6. **No iteration** - Claims everything worked first time
7. **Generic goals** - "Work on robot" instead of specific tasks

### Intervention Strategies

1. **Private conversation** - Ask about specific contributions
2. **Check with teammates** - Verify claimed contributions
3. **Require photo evidence** - Must show themselves working or unique work
4. **Random check-ins** - Ask them to explain work without notes
5. **Increase documentation requirements** - More detailed daily logs
