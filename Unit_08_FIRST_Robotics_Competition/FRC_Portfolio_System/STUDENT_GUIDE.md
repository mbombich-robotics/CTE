# FRC Robotics Project - Student Guide
## Your 9-Week Engineering Journey

---

## Welcome!

For the next 9 weeks, your coursework will be your FIRST Robotics Competition experience. You'll be applying the **engineering design process** to build a competition robot while documenting your individual contributions.

This guide explains what you need to do, when it's due, and how to succeed.

---

## How This Works

You are part of a **subsystem team** responsible for one major component of the robot:

| Team | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Drivetrain | Robot mobility, steering, speed |
| Intake/Collector | Picking up game pieces |
| Shooter/Launcher | Scoring game pieces |
| Climber | End-game hanging mechanism |
| Autonomous/Vision | Camera navigation, auto routines |
| Integration/Electrical | Wiring, pneumatics, systems |

Your grade comes from documenting **YOUR personal contributions** - not just what the team accomplished.

---

## What You'll Turn In

### Every Week: Weekly Reflection (20 points)
**Due: Every Friday**

A short update on what YOU did this week:
- 3+ specific tasks you completed (with dates)
- 2+ photos showing your work
- Challenges you faced and how you solved them
- 3 goals for next week

**Pro tip**: Take photos WHILE you're working. It's hard to recreate evidence later.

---

| Week | Deliverable | Points | What You'll Do |
|------|-------------|--------|----------------|

---

## The 9-Week Schedule

### Weeks 1-2: Research Phase
- Learn the game rules inside and out
- Research how other teams have solved similar problems
- Start brainstorming ideas for your subsystem

### Weeks 3-4: Design Phase
- Sketch and CAD your ideas
- Work with your team to evaluate options
- Make design decisions based on data, not just opinions

### Weeks 5-6: Build & Test Phase
- Get hands-on building prototypes
- Test early and often
- Document failures - they're part of the process!

### Weeks 7-8: Integration & Refinement
- Connect your subsystem to the full robot
- Solve problems that appear during integration
- Fine-tune performance

### Week 9: Competition Prep
- Finalize your portfolio documentation
- Practice your judge presentation
- Get ready to compete!

## Tips for Success

### DO:
- Take photos every day you work
- Write reflections the same day, not days later
- Use specific language: "I wired the limit switch" not "helped with wiring"
- Document failures - judges love seeing iteration
- Ask questions when you're stuck

### DON'T:
- Wait until Friday to remember what you did
- Copy your teammate's reflections
- Say "we" when you mean "I"
- Skip the photo evidence
- Leave things until the last minute

## Important Expectations

### Individual Accountability
This project requires you to prove YOUR contributions. That means:
- Your photos show YOU working or things YOU made
- Your writing uses "I" statements
- You can explain your work if asked
- Your content is different from your teammates

### Professionalism
You're practicing for real engineering work:
- Meet deadlines
- Communicate clearly
- Document thoroughly
- Present confidently

### Collaboration
Working as a team is important, but document your role:
- "I designed the mounting bracket while Alex designed the roller assembly"
- "I wired the motor controllers, and Jordan wrote the code to control them"

## Quick Reference

### Weekly Reflection Checklist
- [ ] 3+ dated contributions using "I" statements
- [ ] 2+ photos of MY work
- [ ] Challenges I faced
- [ ] How I solved them
- [ ] 3 specific goals for next week

### Photo Evidence Ideas
- You at the workstation
- Parts you fabricated
- Wiring you completed
- CAD screenshots
- Test setups
- Before/after comparisons

### Good "I" Statement Examples
- "I designed the intake roller spacing in CAD"
- "I wired the limit switches on the arm"
- "I tested 5 different wheel materials for traction"
- "I wrote the autonomous routine for picking up balls"

### Bad Examples (Avoid These)
- "Helped the team"
- "Worked on the robot"
- "We built stuff"
- "Did some wiring"
