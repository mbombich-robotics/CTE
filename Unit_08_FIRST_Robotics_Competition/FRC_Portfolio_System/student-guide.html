Your 9-Week Engineering Journey

Tips for Success

DO:

  • Take photos every day you work
  • Write reflections the same day
  • Use specific language: "I wired the limit switch"
  • Document failures - judges love iteration!
  • Ask questions when stuck

DON'T:

  • Wait until Friday to remember
  • Copy your teammate's reflections
  • Say "we" when you mean "I"
  • Skip the photo evidence
  • Leave things until last minute

Writing About YOUR Work

Good examples:

  • "I designed the intake roller spacing in CAD"
  • "I wired the limit switches on the arm"
  • "I tested 5 different wheel materials for traction"
  • "I wrote the autonomous routine for picking up balls"

Avoid these:

  • "Helped the team"
  • "Worked on the robot"
  • "We built stuff"
  • "Did some wiring"

Photo Evidence Ideas

  • You at the workstation (have a teammate take it)
  • Parts you fabricated or assembled
  • Wiring you completed
  • CAD screenshots with your name visible
  • Test setups you created
  • Before/after comparisons
Pro tip: Take photos WHILE you're working. It's hard to recreate evidence later. Your phone's camera roll with timestamps is your friend!
