# FIRST Robotics Competition - Course Deliverables
## 9-Week Engineering Design Project

---

## Overview

Students will complete the engineering design process while building a competition robot. Each student is part of a subsystem team and must demonstrate **individual contribution** through weekly reflections and milestone presentations.

### Subsystem Teams (Example)
- **Drivetrain** - Mobility, steering, speed
- **Intake/Collector** - Ball acquisition
- **Shooter/Launcher** - Ball scoring mechanism
- **Climber** - End-game hanging mechanism
- **Autonomous/Vision** - Camera navigation and auto routines
- **Integration/Electrical** - Wiring, pneumatics, system coordination

---

## Weekly Deliverables

### Individual Weekly Reflection (Due every Friday)
**Points: 20 pts/week | Total: 180 pts**

Each student submits a digital reflection containing:

1. **Work Log Summary** (5 pts)
   - What tasks did YOU personally complete this week?
   - Minimum 3 specific contributions with dates

2. **Evidence of Work** (5 pts)
   - Photos/screenshots of YOUR work (minimum 2)
   - Must show you actively participating or your specific contribution

3. **Challenges & Solutions** (5 pts)
   - What problems did you encounter?
   - How did you solve them OR what help do you need?

4. **Next Week's Goals** (5 pts)
   - What do YOU plan to accomplish next week?
   - Be specific and measurable

**Template:**
```
Name: _______________
Date: _______________
Subsystem Team: _______________

MY CONTRIBUTIONS THIS WEEK:
1. [Date] -
2. [Date] -
3. [Date] -

EVIDENCE: [Insert photos/links]

CHALLENGES FACED:


HOW I ADDRESSED THEM:


MY GOALS FOR NEXT WEEK:
1.
2.
3.
```

## Milestone Presentations & Major Deliverables

### Week 1-2: Research & Strategy Phase

#### Deliverable 1: Game Analysis Report (Individual)
**Due: End of Week 1 | Points: 50**

Each student analyzes the game and presents findings:
- Game rules summary (key scoring methods, penalties)
- Strategy analysis: What makes a winning robot?
- Research on 3 mechanisms from previous years (with sources)
- Initial ideas for your subsystem

**Presentation: 3-5 minutes to class**

---

#### Deliverable 2: Subsystem Research Report (Individual within Team)
**Due: End of Week 2 | Points: 50**

Each team member researches a different approach to their subsystem:
- Example: Shooter team - one researches flywheels, one researches pneumatic punchers, one researches catapults
- Pros/cons analysis
- Sketches or diagrams
- Recommendation with justification

**Team Presentation: 5-7 minutes (each member presents their research)**

---

### Week 3-4: Design Phase

#### Deliverable 3: Individual Design Contribution (CAD/Sketch)
**Due: End of Week 3 | Points: 75**

Each student produces:
- Detailed sketches OR CAD models of their design ideas
- Dimensions and materials specified
- Bill of materials estimate
- How this integrates with other subsystems

**Milestone Presentation: Design Review (5 min per team)**
- Present design options to class
- Receive and document feedback
- Each team member must speak

---

#### Deliverable 4: Design Decision Matrix (Team, Individual Sections)
**Due: End of Week 4 | Points: 50**

Team creates decision matrix comparing design options:
- Criteria weighted by importance
- Each team member scores and justifies their portion
- Final design selection with rationale
- Individual section: "My contribution to this decision and why I support it"

---

### Week 5-6: Build & Prototype Phase

#### Deliverable 5: Prototype Documentation (Individual)
**Due: End of Week 5 | Points: 75**

Each student documents their prototype work:
- What did YOU build/fabricate/wire?
- Step-by-step photos of YOUR work
- Problems encountered and solutions
- Initial testing observations

**Milestone Presentation: Prototype Demo (Week 5)**
- Live demonstration of subsystem prototype
- Each team member explains their specific contribution
- 5-7 minutes per team

---

#### Deliverable 6: Testing & Iteration Log (Individual)
**Due: End of Week 6 | Points: 50**

Document testing process:
- Test procedures you developed or executed
- Data collected (measurements, success rates, times)
- Failures documented with photos
- Design changes made based on testing
- Before/after comparison

---

### Week 7-8: Integration & Refinement

#### Deliverable 7: Integration Report (Individual)
**Due: End of Week 7 | Points: 50**

How does your subsystem work with the whole robot?
- Interface points with other subsystems
- Problems discovered during integration
- Your role in solving integration issues
- Wiring diagrams, code snippets, or assembly photos showing your work

**Milestone Presentation: Full Robot Demo (Week 7)**
- Team demonstrates integrated robot
- Each member explains their subsystem's status
- Identify remaining work

---

#### Deliverable 8: Technical Contribution Summary (Individual)
**Due: End of Week 8 | Points: 75**

Comprehensive summary of YOUR technical contributions:
- Complete list of parts you designed/built/programmed
- Skills you developed or applied
- Quantifiable improvements you made (faster cycle time, better accuracy, etc.)
- Photos/videos of your work throughout the project

---

### Week 9: Competition Prep & Presentation

#### Deliverable 9: Engineer Portfolio Entry (Individual)
**Due: End of Week 9 | Points: 100**

Each student creates their section of the Engineer Portfolio:
- Professional formatting
- Clear documentation of design process for YOUR subsystem
- Technical drawings/CAD screenshots
- Testing data and iterations
- Final specifications

---

#### Deliverable 10: Final Presentation - Judge Practice (Individual)
**Due: End of Week 9 | Points: 100**

Each student delivers a 5-minute presentation as if to FIRST judges:
- Explain your subsystem
- Demonstrate technical knowledge
- Answer questions from teacher/classmates
- Professional presentation skills

**Grading Rubric:**
- Technical accuracy (25 pts)
- Communication clarity (25 pts)
- Visual aids/demonstrations (25 pts)
- Q&A responses (25 pts)

## Anti-Coattail Measures

### Individual Accountability Features:
1. **Photo evidence required** - Students must appear in photos OR show work they personally created
2. **Specific contribution language** - "I did X" not "We did X"
3. **Peer evaluation** - End of project, teammates rate each other's contributions (optional addition)
4. **Random check-ins** - Teacher asks individual students to explain their work without notes
5. **Unique research assignments** - Each team member researches different approaches
6. **Individual presentation portions** - Every team member must speak during milestones

### Red Flags to Watch For:
- Vague weekly reflections ("helped the team," "worked on robot")
- No photos of personal work
- Cannot explain technical details when asked
- Same reflection content as teammates
- Missing deadlines while teammates are current

## Notes for Instructor

- **Flexibility**: Adjust due dates based on competition schedule
- **Build season intensity**: Weeks 5-8 may need modified expectations
- **Competition week**: If competition falls during week 9, adjust final presentations
- **Team dynamics**: Watch for teams where one student dominates; redistribute work
- **Skill levels**: Advanced students can mentor, but must document THEIR contributions separately
